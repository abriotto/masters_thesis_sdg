## Task

You will be given the public transcript of one game of One Night Ultimate Werewolf.

You are an external observer, but you must make the voting decision from Team Village's perspective. Based only on the public discussion, decide whether Team Village should eliminate a specific player or if they should execute a "Circle Vote" (nobody dies) because there are no Werewolves in play.

Choose the option that is most likely to help Team Village win.

You are not one of the players.
You do not have any hidden or private information.

## Instructions

- Read the full transcript and the game rules carefully.
- Remember that some roles can change during the Night phase, so a player may believe they are one role while actually ending the Night as another.
- Your justification must support your chosen vote.
- If you believe there are no Werewolves among the active players, Team Village wins by killing nobody. In this case, your chosen vote must be exactly "Circle Vote".
- Do not vote for the center, the Announcer, or any entity that is not a listed player (unless choosing "Circle Vote").

Important:
- Do not assume that a player's starting role is necessarily their final role.
- Night actions are performed according to starting roles, but win conditions depend on final cards.
- Information learned earlier in the Night phase is not automatically updated after later swaps.
- Each player wants their own team to win: Werewolves want to avoid elimination, Village-aligned players want to eliminate a Werewolf if one is present, and the Tanner wants to attract votes in order to be eliminated. Thus, players may bluff or deceive others to achieve their goal.

## Output format

Return valid JSON only.

Use exactly this structure:

{
  "justification": "...",
  "chosen_vote": "..."
}

The "justification" field should contain a concise explanation of the decision in 3-5 sentences.
The value of "chosen_vote" must be either exactly one name from the provided player list, or exactly "Circle Vote" if you believe no Werewolf is present.
Do not include markdown, code fences, analysis, scratchpad text, or text outside the JSON object.
